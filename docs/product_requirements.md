# Synapse Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Overview

Synapse is a Multi-LLM Consensus and Evaluation Platform designed to study how different Large Language Models (LLMs) reason, disagree, influence one another, and converge toward shared conclusions through structured debate.

Unlike traditional LLM evaluation systems that assess models in isolation, Synapse orchestrates interactions between multiple AI systems and measures behavioral dynamics such as agreement, persuasion, confidence shifts, and consensus formation.

The platform serves both as a research environment for analyzing emergent behaviors in AI systems and as an evaluation framework for comparing reasoning patterns across models.

---

# Problem Statement

Current evaluation methods primarily assess Large Language Models independently.

While benchmark scores provide insight into individual model capabilities, they fail to answer important questions regarding collective AI behavior:

* How do different models influence one another?
* Does structured debate improve answer quality?
* Which models are more persuasive during disagreement?
* How does consensus emerge among heterogeneous AI systems?
* Which domains produce the highest disagreement?
* Can multiple models collectively produce better outcomes than a single model?

As multi-agent systems become increasingly common, understanding these dynamics becomes critical for building reliable AI systems.

---

# Vision

To create a research platform that enables the systematic study of reasoning convergence, persuasion dynamics, and consensus formation across multiple AI systems.

Synapse aims to provide insights into how intelligent systems interact, challenge assumptions, exchange information, and collectively arrive at decisions.

---

# Goals

## Primary Goals

* Orchestrate structured debates between multiple LLMs.
* Measure consensus formation across models.
* Analyze changes in model opinions during debate.
* Quantify persuasion and influence dynamics.
* Compare model behavior across different domains.
* Generate interpretable analytics and visualizations.

## Secondary Goals

* Provide a framework for evaluating model reliability.
* Enable reproducible experiments across AI systems.
* Build a dataset of multi-model debate interactions.
* Support future research in multi-agent reasoning.

---

# Non-Goals

Synapse is not intended to be:

* A chatbot replacement.
* A search engine.
* An autonomous AI assistant.
* A task automation platform.
* A prompt engineering playground.
* A model fine-tuning framework.

The primary focus is understanding AI behavior and interaction patterns.

# Core Concepts

## Opinion

The initial position generated by a model when presented with a question.

Example:

Question:
"Should AI-generated code be used in production systems?"

Opinion:
"Yes, with appropriate human review and testing."

---

## Debate

A structured exchange where models critique, challenge, and defend positions.

---

## Consensus

The set of conclusions accepted by multiple participating models after debate rounds.

---

## Persuasion

The measurable influence one model exerts on another during debate.

---

## Opinion Drift

The change in a model's stance between its initial and final responses.

# Functional Requirements

## FR-1: Question Submission

Users must be able to submit a discussion prompt.

Example:

* Ethical questions
* Technical questions
* Business questions
* Healthcare questions

---

## FR-2: Multi-Model Opinion Generation

The system must generate independent responses from each participating model.

Supported Models (MVP):

* Gemini
* GPT-4
* Claude

Future:

* Llama
* DeepSeek
* Mistral
* Qwen

---

## FR-3: Debate Orchestration

The system must facilitate structured debate rounds.

Each model should:

* Review opposing responses.
* Identify weaknesses.
* Defend its position.
* Refine its reasoning.

---

## FR-4: Consensus Generation

The system must generate:

* Agreements
* Disagreements
* Final consensus statement

---

## FR-5: Evaluation Engine

The platform must compute behavioral metrics.

Metrics include:

* Agreement Score
* Persuasion Score
* Opinion Drift
* Confidence Shift
* Debate Efficiency

---

## FR-6: Analytics Dashboard

Users should be able to visualize:

* Model interactions
* Consensus trends
* Influence networks
* Debate timelines

---

# Behavioral Metrics

## Agreement Score

Measures semantic similarity between model conclusions.

Range:

0-100

Where:

* 0 = Complete disagreement
* 100 = Complete agreement

---

## Persuasion Score

Measures how frequently a model causes another model to modify its stance.

---

## Opinion Drift

Measures the distance between initial and final opinions.

---

## Confidence Shift

Tracks changes in model confidence throughout debate rounds.

---

## Debate Efficiency

Measures how quickly consensus is achieved.

---

# MVP Scope (Version 1)

Version 1 will support:

* User question input
* Three participating models
* Initial opinion generation
* One debate round
* Consensus generation
* Agreement scoring
* Basic analytics dashboard

Version 1 will not include:

* User authentication
* Team collaboration
* Experiment scheduling
* Fine-tuning pipelines
* Dataset generation

---

# Success Metrics

The MVP will be considered successful if it can:

* Execute debates across multiple LLMs.
* Produce measurable consensus scores.
* Visualize opinion changes.
* Generate reproducible debate results.
* Demonstrate behavioral differences between models.
