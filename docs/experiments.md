# Synapse Experiments

## Overview

This document defines the experiments conducted using the Synapse platform.

The purpose of these experiments is to study how Large Language Models interact, influence one another, and form consensus through structured debate.

Each experiment is designed to test one or more research hypotheses defined in `research_hypotheses.md`.

---

# Experiment Methodology

Every experiment follows the same structure.

## Experiment Components

### Objective

What question are we trying to answer?

---

### Hypothesis

What do we expect to happen?

---

### Setup

Models, prompts, rounds, and debate configuration.

---

### Metrics

Which evaluation metrics will be analyzed?

Examples:

* Agreement Score
* Opinion Drift
* Persuasion Score
* Confidence Shift
* Debate Efficiency

---

### Results

Quantitative outputs generated by Synapse.

---

### Observations

Interpretation of results.

# Experiment Summary

Experiments transform Synapse from a debate platform into a research environment. By systematically testing hypotheses and collecting behavioral metrics, Synapse enables empirical analysis of consensus formation, persuasion dynamics, and reasoning evolution across Large Language Models.
